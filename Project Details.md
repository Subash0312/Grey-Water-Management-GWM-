# Grey Water Management (GWM)

## Project Title

**Grey Water Management (GWM) – Integrated Grey Water and Black Water Treatment System for Densely Populated Areas with Low Land Requirement**

---

# Project Overview

Grey Water Management (GWM) is a sustainable wastewater treatment system designed to treat both grey water and black water through a compact and integrated treatment process. The project focuses on densely populated residential and urban areas where land availability is limited. The treated water can be reused for non-potable purposes such as toilet flushing, gardening, cleaning, and groundwater recharge, thereby reducing freshwater consumption and environmental pollution.

---

# Problem Statement

Rapid urbanization has increased wastewater generation, while available land for conventional sewage treatment plants is becoming scarce. Most residential areas discharge untreated grey water and black water directly into drains, causing water pollution, health hazards, and depletion of freshwater resources. There is a need for a compact, cost-effective, and efficient wastewater treatment mechanism that can treat both grey water and black water together with minimal land requirement.

# Proposed Solution

The system integrates physical, biological, filtration, and disinfection processes into a compact treatment unit.

Treatment stages include:

1. Screening
2. Sedimentation
3. Biological Treatment
4. Sand Filtration
5. Activated Carbon Filtration
6. Membrane Filtration (Optional)
7. UV/Chlorination Disinfection
8. Water Storage and Reuse

---

# Modules

## Module 1 – Wastewater Collection

Collects grey water and black water from different sources.

## Module 2 – Preliminary Treatment

Removes plastics, debris, grease, and large suspended solids.

## Module 3 – Biological Treatment

Uses microorganisms to decompose organic pollutants.

## Module 4 – Filtration Unit

Removes fine particles, odor, and dissolved impurities.

## Module 5 – Disinfection

Kills bacteria, viruses, and other harmful microorganisms.

## Module 6 – Water Reuse

Stores treated water for non-potable applications.

## Module 7 – Monitoring and Automation (Optional)

Monitors pH, turbidity, flow rate, water level, and treatment efficiency using IoT sensors.
